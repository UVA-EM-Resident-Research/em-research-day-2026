The Point of the Morning

Research in every stage — shared out loud, together.

Research Day 2026 is not a gauntlet. It is a morning for our department to see the breadth of what's underway — protocols still forming, case reports mid-write, QI projects shipping, abstracts accepted at SAEM, and book chapters hitting print.

The format is deliberately mixed: podium talks, posters, lightning rounds, a dress rehearsal for SAEM, and a gallery wall of every accepted abstract from the department. Every graduating PGY-3 presents something. Every faculty mentor gets to see their residents in front of the room.

Come for the full morning if you can. Come for coffee and the gallery if that's what the day allows. Either way — come.

A Word on Faculty Mentorship

This morning is also a celebration of the mentors who made the year possible.

Behind every slide and every poster is a faculty member who took early-morning calls, read late drafts, and pushed questions forward. Research Day is, quietly, for them too.

Faculty mentors — use the full schedule to see exactly where your resident is presenting, and please plan to be in the room for them.
